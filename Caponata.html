Time: 45 mins

Instructions:

  1. Pour the olive oil into a large heavy based saucepan or casserole, place over a medium heat and add the aubergines.
  2. Cook for a good 15-20 mins until they are soft. Scoop the aubergines out of the pan – you should be left with some olive oil.
  3. Add the shallots and cook for about 5 mins until they are soft and translucent. Add the tomatoes and cook slowly, so they break down and turn to a soft mush, then add the aubergines back to the pan.
  4. Now put in the capers, raisins, celery and vinegar, season well and cover with a lid.
  5. Cook over a low heat for 40 mins, until all the vegetables are soft. Stir gently so it doesn’t break up too much; the stew should smell sweet and sour.
  6. When the caponata is cooked, leave to cool slightly while you make the bruschetta.
  7. Heat a griddle pan, drizzle the bread with olive oil and griddle until toasted and lightly charred on both sides, then rub with a garlic clove and season.
  8. Serve the warm caponata scattered with the basil leaves and pine nuts, with bruschetta on the side.

Ingredients

Dough

  • 100ml olive oil
  • 3 large aubergines, cut into 2cm cubes
  • 2 long shallots, chopped
  • 4 large plum tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tsp caper, soaked if salted
  • 50g raisin
  • 4 celery sticks, sliced
  • 50ml red wine vinegar
  • handful toasted pine nuts and basil leaves
  • 8 slices ciabatta
  • olive oil, for drizzling
  • 1 garlic clove
